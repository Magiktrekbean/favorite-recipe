Cake Mix Cookies

These cake mix cookies are simple, delicious, and always a crowd-pleaser. From high school kitchen experiments to a thriving cookie business, I’m here to share my recipe with you!

Why I Love Cake Mix Cookies

Baking Memories One Cookie at a Time

I’ve been making these cake mix cookies since high school, and they’ve always been a hit with friends and family. It’s my go-to recipe when I want something quick, easy, and delicious. The best part? They come together in just a few steps, and you can customize them to match any occasion!

Gather Your Ingredients for a Sweet Treat!

  • Any flavor cake mix
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable oil
  • Optional: 1 cup mix-ins like chocolate chips, M&Ms, or nuts (for added flavor!)

Your Guide to Delicious Cake Mix Cookies

Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven
    Set your oven to 350°F (175°C) to ensure it’s nice and hot by the time your dough is ready.
  2. Mix the Dough
    In a large bowl, combine the cake mix, vegetable oil, and eggs. Stir everything together until the dough is smooth and fully mixed.
  3. Scoop and Shape the Dough
    Scoop tablespoon-sized portions of dough and roll them into balls. Place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, spacing them about 2 inches apart.
  4. Bake
    Pop your cookies in the oven for 8-10 minutes, or until the edges are slightly golden. Don’t worry if the centers still look soft — they’ll firm up as they cool!
  5. Cool and Enjoy!
    Let your cookies cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack. Then, enjoy your warm, chewy cake mix cookies!

Secrets for Cookie Success!

  • Use a hand mixer for a super smooth dough — it makes the process so much easier!
  • Feel free to get creative with your mix-ins! It’s the perfect chance to customize these cookies to your liking.
  • For extra soft cookies, pull them out right when the edges turn golden but the center is still a bit soft. They’ll firm up perfectly as they cool!
  • Keep any leftovers in an airtight container to maintain that fresh, soft texture.
  • You can freeze the dough for later use! Just scoop the dough into balls, freeze them on a baking sheet, and store the frozen dough balls in a bag for up to 3 months. Bake them straight from the freezer — they’ll still be delicious!
